Beginners Guide For Periods

What is a period and When does it happen and how often?

  • Period is the monthly release of blood from the vagina.
  • Part of the menstrual cycle involving egg release and uterus lining thickening.
  • If no fertilization, egg breaks down, and uterus lining sheds.
  • Result: bloody fluid and tissue flow out through the vagina.
  • Commonly referred to as menstruation or a period.
  • Girls typically get their first period around age 12 (8 - 16 years range).
  • Periods usually last about five days, but duration can vary.
  • Initial periods may be light, and regularity may take time to establish.

PMS (premenstrual syndrome)

  • Hormone fluctuations before periods can lead to PMS/PMT.
  • Not all periods or individuals experience PMS.
  • Common symptoms: bloating, breast tenderness, irritability, mood swings, skin breakouts.
  • Symptoms typically improve after period starts and last a few days.

Spotting

  • Vaginal bleeding that comes when you don't expect it, based on your typical menstrual cycle, can be a cause of inconvenience, discomfort and worry.
  • Among the things that can cause spotting are:
  1. Sexual activity
  2. Cervical screening
  3. Imbalance in the hormones that regulate your cycle (oestrogen and progesterone)
  4. Infection such as bacterial vaginosis, yeast, chlamydia, gonorrhoea or trichomoniasis
  5. Cancer of the ovaries, uterus, cervix or vagina
